The 5-Step Integrity Check

Nairobi's market for counterfeit spirits is unfortunately sophisticated, but a discerning eye can spot a 'fake' Jameson or Glenfiddich in seconds. Here is what our logistics team looks for during intake:

  • 1. The Seal Check: Authentic bottles have a 'KRA Stamp' that is properly aligned and perfectly printed. If the stamp looks blurry or easily peels off, stay away.
  • 2. The Bubble Test: Shake the bottle. In high-proof spirits, the 'beads' or bubbles should disappear almost instantly. If they linger for more than 5 seconds, the liquid may contain additives or high sugar content.
  • 3. Laser Coding: Premium brands like Johnnie Walker laser-etch coding onto the glass, usually near the base. Scammers often forget this or use cheap ink that can be rubbed off.
  • 4. Label Glue: Look at the back of the label through the glass. Genuine bottles are labeled by precision machines and use uniform glue stripes. Messy, manual gluing is a sign of refilling.
